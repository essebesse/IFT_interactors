2. KNOWN CILIARY ROLE AND MOLECULAR FUNCTION
================================================================================

PRIMARY FUNCTION - Inner Junction Structural Protein:
CFAP20 is a core component of the inner junction (IJ) of axonemal doublet
microtubules. Together with PACRG (Parkin Co-Regulated Gene protein), CFAP20
forms the inner junction bridge that links protofilament A1 of the A-tubule
to protofilament B10 of the B-tubule in all nine doublet microtubules.

Structural Organization:
  - PACRG and CFAP20 are arranged in an alternating pattern
  - Forms repeating units along the entire length of each doublet microtubule
  - Distinct from the outer junction (tripartite tubulin connections)
  - Non-tubulin structural element bridging A- and B-tubules

Key Molecular Functions:

1. STRUCTURAL INTEGRITY:
   - Maintains doublet microtubule stability and integrity
   - Prevents axonemal disintegration during ciliary beating
   - Critical for both motile and non-motile cilia structure

2. MICROTUBULE DYNAMICS:
   - FAP20 recruits free tubulin to the existing microtubule lattice
   - Functions independent of GTP
   - Participates in microtubule lattice assembly at inner junction

3. CILIARY MOTILITY REGULATION:
   - Required for proper ciliary beat pattern
   - Modulates planar asymmetrical waveform in motile cilia
   - Acts as hub for regulating dynein-driven microtubule sliding
   - Influences radial spoke (RS) and Nexin-Dynein Regulatory Complex (N-DRC)

4. TUBULIN POST-TRANSLATIONAL MODIFICATIONS:
   - Required for axonemal microtubule polyglutamylation
   - Inner junction serves as interaction hub involving tubulin PTMs

5. CILIA SIZE AND MORPHOLOGY:
   - Regulates ciliary size and morphology
   - Essential for proper axonemal architecture

================================================================================
3. CILIARY LOCALIZATION
================================================================================

Primary Localization: AXONEME INNER JUNCTION

Specific Compartments:
  - Inner junction of doublet microtubules (all 9 doublets)
  - Runs along entire length of each doublet microtubule
  - Present in both motile and non-motile cilia
  - Also localizes to daughter centriole

Subcellular Distribution:
  - Cilium-specific protein
  - Localization unaltered in BROMI-KO, CCRK-KO, or ICK-KO cells
  - Direct localization to axonemal structure (not dependent on IFT
    regulatory proteins for positioning)

NOT a Traditional IFT Cargo:
CFAP20 is an axonemal structural protein that is transported TO the cilium
as cargo during ciliogenesis, but once incorporated, becomes a permanent
structural component of the doublet microtubules. CFAP20 is NOT continuously
transported like dynamic IFT proteins.

Role in IFT Turnaround:
Despite being a structural axonemal protein, CFAP20 participates in regulating
IFT turnaround at the ciliary tip:
  - Interacts with BROMI/TBC1D32 and CCRK/CDK20
  - Part of regulatory system controlling IFT machinery direction change
  - Works with ICK/CILK1 kinase pathway
  - Regulatory role separate from its structural function

4. KNOWN PROTEIN INTERACTIONS
================================================================================

PRIMARY INTERACTION PARTNERS:

1. PACRG (Parkin Co-Regulated Gene Protein):
   - Direct interaction partner forming inner junction core
   - Alternating arrangement along doublet microtubules
   - Both required for inner junction integrity
   - Co-localization throughout axoneme length
   Reference: PMID 31116684 (Mol Biol Cell, 2019)

2. TUBULIN (α/β-tubulin heterodimers):
   - FAP20 recruits free tubulin to microtubule lattice
   - GTP-independent tubulin binding
   - Flexible interactions in microtubule:FAP20:tubulin complex
   Reference: PMID 37816351 (Structure, 2023)

3. CFAP52:
   - Identified as axonemal interactor via co-IP in HEK293T cells
   - Both components of inner junction region
   - Part of inner junction interaction network

4. ENKUR:
   - Core component of inner junction complex
   - Co-localizes with CFAP20 and PACRG

IFT-RELATED INTERACTIONS:

5. BROMI/TBC1D32:
   - Direct or indirect interaction
   - Regulates IFT turnaround process
   - Part of CCRK/ICK regulatory pathway

6. CCRK/CDK20:
   - Indirect interaction via BROMI
   - IFT turnaround regulation
   - Controls ICK kinase activation

7. FAM149B1/JBTS36:
   - Interactome studies suggest interaction with BROMI and CFAP20
   - IFT turnaround pathway component

FUNCTIONAL ASSOCIATIONS (Secondary Interactions):

8. Radial Spokes (RS):
   - Inner junction proteins influence RS assembly/function
   - CFAP20 and PACRG affect ciliary beat via RS modulation

9. Nexin-Dynein Regulatory Complex (N-DRC):
   - Inner junction influences N-DRC function
   - Regulates motility patterns

10. Inner Dynein Arms (IDA):
    - Reduced assembly of IDA b in PACRG/FAP20 mutants
    - Inner junction required for proper dynein arm positioning

11. Outer Dynein Arms (ODA):
    - Co-IP studies identify ODA components as CFAP20 interactors
    - Indirect effects on ODA assembly/stability

Connection to Predicted IFT57 Interaction:
IFT57 is a core component of the IFT-B2 complex (along with IFT20, IFT38,
IFT54, IFT80, IFT172). The predicted CFAP20-IFT57 interaction (ipSAE=0.628)
is biologically plausible for several reasons:

  1. CARGO TRANSPORT: CFAP20 must be transported to the axoneme as cargo
     during ciliogenesis. IFT57 is part of the IFT-B complex responsible
     for anterograde transport of axonemal precursors.

  2. IFT TURNAROUND: CFAP20 participates in IFT turnaround regulation at
     the ciliary tip, potentially interacting with IFT-B components during
     this process.

  3. COMPLEX STABILIZATION: IFT57 stabilizes the assembled IFT complex and
     mediates transport of motility-related flagellar cargo - precisely the
     category CFAP20 falls into.

  4. SPATIAL PROXIMITY: Both proteins function at the ciliary tip during
     IFT turnaround and cargo delivery processes.

However, this interaction is likely TRANSIENT during:
  - Initial transport of CFAP20 to axoneme during ciliogenesis
  - IFT turnaround regulation at ciliary tip
  - Cargo loading/unloading processes

Once incorporated into inner junction, CFAP20 becomes a stable structural
component and would no longer interact with IFT machinery.

5. DISEASE ASSOCIATIONS
================================================================================

CONFIRMED HUMAN DISEASE:

1. RETINAL DYSTROPHY (Primary Association)
   OMIM: Associated with CFAP20 mutations

   Clinical Features:
   - Inherited retinal dystrophy (IRD)
   - Visual deficit presentation: adolescence to adulthood (17-56 years)
   - Progressive retinal degeneration
   - Affects photoreceptor primary cilia (non-motile sensory cilia)

   Genetic Findings (8 patients from 4 unrelated families):
   - Biallelic CFAP20 missense mutations
   - Canonical splice-site variants
   - Identified mutations:
     * p.(Arg102His)
     * p.(Arg113Trp)
     * p.(Arg153Gly)
     * p.(Tyr86Cys) - most damaging, associated with syndromic IRD

   Pathomechanism:
   - CFAP20 required for photoreceptor outer segment function
   - Disrupted connecting cilium (modified primary cilium)
   - Impaired protein transport in photoreceptor cells
   - Represents novel pathomechanism: inner junction dysfunction in
     non-motile sensory cilia

   Reference: PMID 36329026 (Nature Communications, 2022)
   DOI: 10.1038/s41467-022-33820-w

2. PRIMARY CILIARY DYSKINESIA (PCD) - Candidate Gene
   Status: Strong candidate, not yet definitively confirmed

   Rationale:
   - CFAP20 essential for motile cilia function in zebrafish
   - Inner junction disruption affects ciliary beating
   - Structural homology to known PCD genes
   - Critical for respiratory cilia motility

   Expected PCD Features if confirmed:
   - Neonatal respiratory distress
   - Recurrent upper and lower respiratory infections
   - Chronic rhinosinusitis
   - Bronchiectasis
   - Potential laterality defects (situs abnormalities)
   - Male subfertility/infertility

   Reference: PMID 36329026 (Nature Communications, 2022)

RELATED CILIOPATHIES (Other CFAP Genes):

3. Male Infertility - Multiple Morphological Abnormalities of Sperm
   Flagella (MMAF)

   Related CFAP genes causing MMAF:
   - CFAP43 and CFAP44: 20.5% of MMAF cohort (78 patients)
     Reference: PMID 28552195
   - CFAP251: 5% of Han Chinese MMAF cohort (65 patients)
     Reference: PMID 30310178
   - CFAP61: Biallelic variants cause MMAF
   - CFAP52: Defects in sperm head-tail connection

   Note on CFAP20 and Male Fertility:
   While CFAP20 is expressed in sperm flagella and is part of the inner
   junction complex essential for flagellar structure, no human CFAP20
   mutations have been definitively linked to male infertility to date.
   However, given:
   - Its critical role in flagellar structure/stability
   - Expression in sperm flagella
   - Phenotypes of related CFAP genes
   - Conservation of function across motile cilia

   CFAP20 remains a strong candidate for future investigation in male
   infertility cases with flagellar defects.

Disease Panel Classification:
- UK Genomics England PanelApp: CFAP20 listed in "Retinal disorders" panel
- Strong evidence for retinal dystrophy
- Emerging evidence for motile ciliopathies

8. AXONEME ASSEMBLY FUNCTION
================================================================================

CFAP20 ROLE IN AXONEME ASSEMBLY:

A. STRUCTURAL ASSEMBLY FUNCTION

Inner Junction Assembly:
  - CFAP20 and PACRG are core building blocks of inner junction
  - Alternating arrangement creates continuous bridge
  - Links protofilament A1 (A-tubule) to B10 (B-tubule)
  - Essential for completing doublet microtubule structure

Assembly Model (Based on Structural Studies):
  1. A-tubule forms as complete 13-protofilament microtubule
  2. B-tubule forms as incomplete 10-protofilament structure
  3. CFAP20 recruits tubulin to microtubule lattice (GTP-independent)
  4. PACRG and CFAP20 alternately bridge A1 and B10 protofilaments
  5. Inner junction stabilizes the A-B tubule connection
  6. Outer junction (tubulin-tubulin) provides additional connection

Reference: PMID 37816351 (Structure, 2023)

B. DOUBLET MICROTUBULE COMPLETION

Critical Role:
- Without CFAP20, B-tubule fails to properly connect to A-tubule
- Inner junction absence leads to axonemal instability
- Doublets may partially disassemble during ciliary beating
- Progressive structural deterioration in CFAP20 mutants

Evidence from Chlamydomonas:
- FAP20 mutant axonemes disintegrate during beating
- Demonstrates inner junction essential for mechanical integrity
- Not just motility, but structural maintenance role

C. TRANSPORT TO ASSEMBLY SITE

IFT-Mediated Transport:
CFAP20 itself must be transported to ciliary assembly site via IFT:
  - Synthesized in cytoplasm
  - Loaded onto IFT trains at ciliary base
  - Transported anterogradely to ciliary tip by IFT-B/kinesin-2
  - Released at ciliary tip for assembly into growing axoneme
  - Incorporated into inner junction at doublet microtubule assembly site

Connection to IFT57:
IFT57 (IFT-B2 complex) role in CFAP20 transport:
  - IFT57 stabilizes assembled IFT complex
  - Mediates transport of motility-related flagellar cargo
  - CFAP20 qualifies as "motility-related flagellar cargo"
  - Predicted interaction may reflect cargo-transporter relationship

D. SECONDARY EFFECTS ON OTHER AXONEMAL COMPONENTS

When PACRG/FAP20 are absent or mutated:
  1. Reduced Inner Dynein Arm assembly (IDA b)
  2. Impaired beak-MIP structures
  3. Altered Radial Spoke assembly/positioning
  4. Effects on Nexin-Dynein Regulatory Complex
  5. Indirect effects on Outer Dynein Arms

Hub Function:
Inner junction serves as assembly hub for multiple axonemal components:
  - Provides attachment points for regulatory complexes
  - Coordinates proper spacing of axonemal structures
  - Integrates multiple functional modules (dyneins, radial spokes, N-DRC)

E. DIFFERENTIAL ASSEMBLY IN MOTILE VS NON-MOTILE CILIA

Motile Cilia (9+2 axoneme):
  - Full complement of dynein arms
  - Radial spokes and central pair
  - CFAP20 required for proper beat pattern
  - Complex regulatory network assembly

Non-Motile Cilia (9+0 axoneme):
  - No dynein arms or central pair
  - CFAP20 still essential for structural integrity
  - Critical for sensory signaling functions
  - Demonstrates role beyond motility machinery

Key Insight:
CFAP20 function in non-motile sensory cilia (C. elegans, human
photoreceptors) reveals that inner junction is FUNDAMENTAL to ciliary
architecture, not just an accessory to motility apparatus.

F. TUBULIN POST-TRANSLATIONAL MODIFICATIONS

CFAP20 Required for:
  - Axonemal microtubule polyglutamylation
  - Inner junction as PTM hub
  - Proper tubulin modification patterns essential for:
    * Dynein arm attachment
    * Motor protein regulation
    * Ciliary protein trafficking

G. AXONEME MAINTENANCE (Beyond Initial Assembly)

Continuous Function:
  - CFAP20 not just for initial assembly
  - Ongoing structural maintenance required
  - Prevents disassembly during mechanical stress of beating
  - Recruits tubulin for repair/maintenance

Chlamydomonas Evidence:
  - Mutant flagella progressively disintegrate
  - Demonstrates continuous structural support role
  - Not static scaffolding, but active maintenance function

ASSEMBLY TIMELINE:

During Ciliogenesis:
  1. Basal body maturation
  2. IFT machinery assembles at ciliary base
  3. Tubulin transported to tip via IFT
  4. CFAP20/PACRG transported as cargo via IFT-B (likely IFT57 involvement)
  5. Doublet microtubules assemble at ciliary tip
  6. CFAP20/PACRG incorporated into growing inner junction
  7. Secondary complexes (dyneins, radial spokes) added to stabilized doublets

Mature Cilium:
  8. CFAP20 provides ongoing structural support
  9. Facilitates tubulin recruitment for maintenance
  10. Serves as hub for regulatory protein interactions

9. IFT57 CONNECTION AND BIOLOGICAL SIGNIFICANCE
================================================================================

PREDICTED INTERACTION ANALYSIS:

AlphaFold3 Prediction:
- CFAP20 ↔ IFT57 interaction
- ipSAE confidence: 0.628 (medium confidence)
- Interaction context: IFT-B2 complex member with axonemal cargo

IFT57 BACKGROUND:

Protein Identity:
- Core component of IFT-B2 subcomplex
- IFT-B2 members: IFT20, IFT38, IFT54, IFT57, IFT80, IFT172
- Contains calponin homology (CH) domain and predicted coiled-coils
- Located at interface between IFT-B1 and IFT-B2 subcomplexes

IFT57 Functions:
  1. Stabilizes assembled IFT complex
  2. Prevents IFT complex degradation
  3. Mediates transport of motility-related flagellar cargo
  4. Bridges IFT-B1 and IFT-B2 subcomplexes
  5. Does NOT directly bind tubulin cargo (IFT54 does this)
  6. CH domain mediates interaction with IFT172

Reference: PMID 26912722; PMID 28104816

BIOLOGICAL RATIONALE FOR CFAP20-IFT57 INTERACTION:

1. CARGO TRANSPORT RELATIONSHIP:

Direct Cargo Transport:
- CFAP20 must be transported from cytoplasm to cilium during ciliogenesis
- IFT-B complex (including IFT57) performs anterograde transport
- IFT57 specifically transports motility-related flagellar proteins
- CFAP20 is a motility-related axonemal structural protein
- Temporal interaction during cargo loading, transport, and delivery

Cargo Binding Mechanism:
- IFT57 does not bind cargo directly
- IFT-B2 subcomplex binds tubulin via IFT54
- CFAP20 may bind IFT57 as:
  a) Direct cargo-adaptor interaction
  b) Part of larger cargo complex with tubulin
  c) Stabilization during transport process

2. IFT TURNAROUND REGULATION:

CFAP20 Role in IFT Turnaround:
- CFAP20 interacts with BROMI/TBC1D32 and CCRK/CDK20
- Part of regulatory system controlling IFT direction at ciliary tip
- Works with ICK/CILK1 kinase pathway

Potential IFT57 Interaction at Ciliary Tip:
- IFT complex arrives at ciliary tip via anterograde transport
- Must transition from anterograde to retrograde transport
- CFAP20 (as structural axonemal component) may:
  a) Provide docking site for IFT complex at tip
  b) Facilitate cargo release from IFT-B
  c) Participate in turnaround regulatory signaling
- IFT57 as IFT complex component would interact during this process

3. SPATIAL AND TEMPORAL PROXIMITY:

During Ciliogenesis:
- CFAP20 synthesized in cytoplasm
- Must reach ciliary tip for incorporation into doublet microtubules
- IFT57 in IFT trains traveling to tip
- High probability of interaction during this phase

At Ciliary Tip:
- Site of axoneme assembly
- Site of IFT turnaround
- CFAP20 delivered and incorporated
- IFT57 undergoes complex remodeling for retrograde transport
- Transient interactions likely

4. PROTEIN COMPLEX ASSEMBLY:

CFAP20 as Part of Larger Complex:
- CFAP20 delivered with PACRG
- May be co-transported with other MIPs (microtubule inner proteins)
- Inner junction components may form pre-assembly complex
- IFT57 could interact with complex rather than CFAP20 alone

5. FUNCTIONAL VALIDATION FROM RELATED STUDIES:

IFT-B2 and Tubulin Transport:
- IFT-B2 binds αβ-tubulin via IFT54 CH domain
- CFAP20 recruits free tubulin to microtubule lattice
- Both proteins involved in tubulin dynamics
- May function in coordinated pathway:
  IFT-B2 delivers tubulin → CFAP20 incorporates into inner junction

IFT57 and Motility Proteins:
- IFT57 knock-down affects motility-related protein transport
- CFAP20 is essential motility-related structural protein
- Direct evidence IFT57 transports proteins like CFAP20

INTERACTION MODEL:

TRANSIENT vs STABLE INTERACTION:
The CFAP20-IFT57 interaction is likely TRANSIENT, occurring during:

Phase 1 - Cargo Loading (Ciliary Base):
  - CFAP20 synthesized and enters ciliary compartment
  - Binds to IFT-B complex (potentially via IFT57)
  - Cargo-adaptor association formed

Phase 2 - Anterograde Transport (Along Axoneme):
  - IFT train with CFAP20 cargo moves toward tip
  - IFT57 helps stabilize cargo-IFT complex
  - Interaction maintained during transport

Phase 3 - Cargo Release (Ciliary Tip):
  - IFT complex arrives at tip
  - CFAP20 released from IFT-B
  - Incorporated into assembling inner junction
  - IFT57-CFAP20 interaction broken

Phase 4 - IFT Turnaround (Ciliary Tip):
  - CFAP20 (now incorporated in axoneme) may interact with IFT proteins
  - Regulatory function in turnaround process
  - Brief re-association possible

Phase 5 - Mature Cilium (Axoneme):
  - CFAP20 stably incorporated in inner junction
  - No ongoing IFT57 interaction
  - CFAP20 now structural, not cargo

ALTERNATIVE EXPLANATIONS FOR PREDICTED INTERACTION:

1. Evolutionary Conservation of Binding Interfaces:
   - Both highly conserved proteins
   - May share ancient protein-protein interaction domains
   - Structural similarity leading to predicted interaction

2. Indirect Interaction via Intermediary:
   - Both may bind common protein (e.g., BROMI, tubulin)
   - AlphaFold3 detects indirect association
   - Not direct physical contact

3. Regulatory Interaction:
   - Post-translational modification of one by the other
   - Signaling cascade involvement
   - Allosteric regulation

EXPERIMENTAL VALIDATION NEEDED:

To confirm CFAP20-IFT57 interaction:
  1. Co-immunoprecipitation from ciliated cell lysates
  2. Proximity ligation assay (PLA) in cilia
  3. Yeast two-hybrid or mammalian two-hybrid
  4. Surface plasmon resonance (binding affinity)
  5. Cryo-EM of IFT trains with bound cargo
  6. Fluorescence microscopy co-localization during ciliogenesis
  7. Cross-linking mass spectrometry

Key Question:
Is the interaction constitutive or cell cycle/ciliogenesis-dependent?
Prediction: Interaction strongest during active ciliogenesis, weak or
absent in mature cilia.

BIOLOGICAL SIGNIFICANCE OF INTERACTION:

If Confirmed, Would Reveal:
  1. Specific cargo adaptor for inner junction proteins
  2. Novel IFT-B cargo binding mechanism
  3. Link between IFT transport and axoneme assembly
  4. Regulatory checkpoint in ciliogenesis
  5. Potential therapeutic target for ciliopathies

Clinical Relevance:
- Mutations affecting CFAP20-IFT57 interaction could impair:
  * CFAP20 delivery to cilia
  * Inner junction assembly
  * Axoneme structural integrity
  * Ciliary motility and sensory functions
- May explain some ciliopathy cases without coding mutations

SUMMARY OF IFT57 CONNECTION:
The predicted CFAP20-IFT57 interaction (ipSAE=0.628) is biologically
plausible and likely reflects:
  - Cargo-transporter relationship during ciliogenesis
  - Transient association during CFAP20 delivery to cilium
  - Possible regulatory interaction at ciliary tip (IFT turnaround)
  - Functional link between IFT transport and axoneme assembly

Medium confidence score (0.628) appropriate for transient interaction that
occurs during specific cellular processes rather than constitutive binding.

  1. Essential structural role that cannot tolerate variation

  3. Fundamental importance to ciliary architecture
  4. Strong purifying selection across eukaryotic evolution

Present in All Organisms with Motile Cilia/Flagella:
  - Green algae (Chlamydomonas)

maintained in all ciliated eukaryotes. Loss of FAP20 correlates with loss
of cilia.

12. SUMMARY AND CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

CFAP20 (Cilia and Flagella Associated Protein 20) is a HIGHLY CONSERVED and
ESSENTIAL structural component of the ciliary axoneme, specifically forming
the core of the inner junction that bridges the A- and B-tubules of doublet
microtubules.

KEY FINDINGS:

Structure and Localization:
  - Core inner junction protein (alternating with PACRG)
  - Links protofilament A1 to B10 in all nine doublet microtubules
  - Present in both motile and non-motile cilia
  - 89% identical between Chlamydomonas and human (extraordinary conservation)

Molecular Functions:
  - Maintains doublet microtubule structural integrity
  - Recruits tubulin to microtubule lattice (GTP-independent)
  - Regulates ciliary beat pattern and waveform
  - Serves as hub for axonemal protein interactions
  - Required for tubulin post-translational modifications
  - Participates in IFT turnaround regulation at ciliary tip

Human Disease:
  - CONFIRMED: Retinal dystrophy (biallelic mutations in 8 patients)
  - CANDIDATE: Primary ciliary dyskinesia
  - POTENTIAL: Male infertility (sperm flagellar defects)

Model Organism Phenotypes:
  - Zebrafish: Retinal degeneration + motile cilia defects
  - C. elegans: Sensory cilia structural defects, behavioral abnormalities
  - Chlamydomonas: Abnormal flagellar waveform, axonemal instability

Predicted IFT57 Interaction (ipSAE=0.628):
  - Biologically plausible cargo-transporter relationship
  - CFAP20 must be transported to cilium via IFT-B complex
  - IFT57 mediates transport of motility-related flagellar cargo
  - Likely transient interaction during ciliogenesis and cargo delivery
  - Possible regulatory interaction at ciliary tip (IFT turnaround)

Clinical Significance:
  - CFAP20 should be included in genetic screening for:
    * Inherited retinal dystrophy
    * Primary ciliary dyskinesia (candidate)
    * Male infertility with flagellar defects
  - High conservation suggests mutations highly penetrant
  - Inner junction dysfunction represents novel ciliopathy mechanism
  - Affects both motile and sensory cilia functions

BIOLOGICAL SIGNIFICANCE OF CFAP20-IFT57 INTERACTION:

The medium-confidence prediction (ipSAE=0.628) likely reflects a TRANSIENT
but FUNCTIONALLY IMPORTANT interaction occurring during:
  1. CFAP20 transport as IFT cargo during ciliogenesis
  2. Cargo delivery at the ciliary tip
  3. IFT turnaround regulation
  4. Coordinated axoneme assembly

This interaction connects two fundamental ciliary processes:
  - IFT-mediated protein transport (IFT57/IFT-B complex)
  - Axonemal structural assembly (CFAP20/inner junction)

Understanding this interaction may reveal:
  - Specific cargo adaptor mechanisms for inner junction proteins
  - Regulatory checkpoints in ciliogenesis
  - Potential therapeutic targets for ciliopathies
  - Novel disease mechanisms affecting both IFT and axoneme assembly

FUTURE DIRECTIONS:

Research Priorities:
  1. Validate CFAP20-IFT57 interaction experimentally
  2. Screen PCD cohorts for CFAP20 mutations
  3. Investigate CFAP20 in male infertility cases
  4. Generate mouse knockout model
  5. Structural studies of CFAP20 in complex with IFT machinery
  6. Therapeutic strategies targeting inner junction stability

Clinical Applications:
  - Expand genetic testing panels to include CFAP20
  - Develop targeted therapies for inner junction stabilization
  - Genetic counseling for CFAP20 mutation carriers
  - Prenatal/preimplantation genetic diagnosis

FINAL ASSESSMENT:

CFAP20 represents a critical and ancient ciliary component whose dysfunction
causes disease through a novel mechanism: inner junction disruption affecting
both motile and sensory cilia. The predicted interaction with IFT57 highlights
the interconnection between ciliary transport and structural assembly,
warranting further investigation to fully understand ciliogenesis and
ciliopathy pathogenesis.
