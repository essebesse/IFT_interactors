1. PROTEIN IDENTITY & BASIC INFORMATION
================================================================================

Gene Name: TIMM50
Alternative Names: TIM50, Translocase of inner mitochondrial membrane 50
UniProt ID: Q3ZCQ8 (human)
OMIM ID: *607381 (gene), #617698 (disease - MGCA9)
Chromosome Location: 19q13

Primary Function:
TIMM50 is a core receptor subunit of the TIM23 complex in the mitochondrial
inner membrane, responsible for recognizing and importing proteins with
mitochondrial targeting sequences (presequences) into the mitochondrial matrix
and inner membrane.

Cellular Localization:
- Mitochondrial inner membrane (transmembrane protein)
- Large intermembrane space (IMS) domain exposed to IMS
- N-terminus in mitochondrial matrix

================================================================================
2. MITOCHONDRIAL FUNCTION: TIM23 COMPLEX & PROTEIN IMPORT MECHANISM
================================================================================

2.1 TIM23 COMPLEX ARCHITECTURE
------------------------------

Core Components:
- TIMM23: Forms structural platform for complex assembly
- TIMM17A/B: Forms the actual protein translocation channel/pore
- TIMM50: Receptor subunit in intermembrane space

The TIM23 complex exists in two functional forms:

1. TIM23SORT: For lateral insertion of single/dual-pass proteins into inner
   membrane
   - Associates with TIMM21 (TIM21) and ROMO1 (Mgr2 in yeast)

2. TIM23MOTOR: For complete translocation of proteins into mitochondrial matrix
   - Associates with PAM (Presequence translocase-associated motor) complex
   - PAM components: TIMM44 (Tim44), mtHsp70, TIMM16 (Pam16), TIMM17 (Pam17),
     TIMM18 (Pam18), and MgeI

REFERENCE: Nature Communications 2021; doi:10.1038/s41467-021-26016-1
PMID: 34593807 - "Mapping protein interactions in the active TOM-TIM23
supercomplex"

2.2 PROTEIN IMPORT MECHANISM
----------------------------

Step-by-step process:

1. PRESEQUENCE RECOGNITION:
   - TIMM50 recognizes mitochondrial targeting signal (presequence) on cargo
   - Tim50 functions as the receptor subunit that recognizes the mitochondrial
     targeting signal on protein cargo destined for the mitochondrial inner
     membrane and matrix

2. PROTEIN TRANSFER FROM TOM TO TIM23:
   - TIMM50, in cooperation with TIMM23, facilitates transfer of translocating
     protein from TOM (outer membrane) complex to TIM23 complex
   - TOM40 complex in outer membrane hands off preproteins to TIM23

3. CHANNEL OPENING/CLOSING:
   - Hydrophilic cis domain of TIMM50 maintains permeability barrier of
     mitochondria by closing the translocation pore in presequence-regulated
     manner
   - In absence of preproteins, Tim50 closes Tim23 channel to prevent proton
     leakage

4. LATERAL SORTING vs COMPLETE TRANSLOCATION:

   LATERAL SORTING (TIM23SORT pathway):
   - TIM21 displaces PAM complex component Pam18
   - TIM23 channel opens laterally
   - Translocating protein inserted into mitochondrial inner membrane
   - Laterally released substrates are MOST SENSITIVE to loss of TIMM50

   COMPLETE TRANSLOCATION (TIM23MOTOR pathway):
   - Tim44 and mtHsp70 of PAM complex engage polypeptide at exit of Tim23
     channel
   - Motor proteins pull protein completely into matrix

5. MOTOR COORDINATION:
   - Tim23-Tim50 interactions facilitate late step of protein translocation by
     promoting motor functions of mitochondrial Hsp70 in the matrix

REFERENCE: Cell 2002; 111(4):507-518
PMID: 12437924 - "Tim50 Is a Subunit of the TIM23 Complex that Links Protein
Translocation across the Outer and Inner Mitochondrial Membranes"

REFERENCE: Journal of Cell Biology 2007; 176(5):613-624
PMID: 17325205 - "Tim23-Tim50 pair coordinates functions of translocators and
motor proteins in mitochondrial protein import"

REFERENCE: Autophagy 2024; 20(6):1311-1328
PMID: 38828998 - "Reduced Protein Import via TIM23 SORT Drives Disease
Pathology in TIMM50-Associated Mitochondrial Disease"

2.3 RECENT STRUCTURAL INSIGHTS (2024)
-------------------------------------

Surprising discovery: The cavity of TIMM17, NOT TIMM23, forms the actual
protein translocation path, whereas TIMM23 likely plays a structural role.

This overturns previous assumptions about which subunit forms the channel.

REFERENCE: Nature Communications 2024; doi:10.1038/s41467-024-53573-1
"Structural basis of mitochondrial protein import by the TIM23 complex"

3. STRUCTURAL BIOLOGY: DOMAINS & 3D ARCHITECTURE
================================================================================

3.1 DOMAIN ORGANIZATION
-----------------------

Human TIMM50 contains three main domains:

1. MITOCHONDRIAL TARGETING SEQUENCE (MTS)
   - Directs protein to mitochondria during biosynthesis

2. TRANSMEMBRANE DOMAIN (TMD)
   - Single-pass transmembrane helix
   - Anchors protein in inner membrane
   - Orients C-terminal domain to intermembrane space

3. INTERMEMBRANE SPACE (IMS) DOMAIN
   - Large hydrophilic domain
   - Contains two sub-domains:

   a) CORE DOMAIN (amino acids 164-361 in yeast)
      - FCP1-like phosphatase fold (disputed functional significance)
      - Interacts with TIMM23 and TIMM21
      - Crystal structure solved to 1.83 A resolution

   b) PRESEQUENCE-BINDING DOMAIN (PBD, amino acids 395-476 in yeast)
      - Contains large groove as putative binding site for presequences
      - Acidic groove for positively charged presequences

3.2 KEY STRUCTURAL FEATURES
---------------------------

PROTRUDING β-HAIRPIN:
- Exposed β-hairpin crucial for interaction with TIMM23
- Contains critical residues R214 and K217
- Mutations in β-hairpin (R214, K217) strongly reduce ability to bind TIMM23
- Proximal to acidic presequence-binding groove

PHOSPHATASE-LIKE FOLD:
- Crystal structure revealed FCP1 phosphatase-like fold
- Human TIMM50 possesses dual-specific phosphatase activity in vitro
- HOWEVER: Lacks canonical D-x-D-x-[TV] active site
- Physiological relevance of phosphatase activity disputed
- May not display phosphatase activity in vivo

3.3 CARDIOLIPIN INTERACTIONS
----------------------------

CRITICAL REGULATORY MECHANISM:

The soluble receptor domain of TIMM50 interacts with membranes and with
specific sites on the TIMM23 channel in a manner directly modulated by
CARDIOLIPIN (a phospholipid enriched in mitochondrial inner membrane).

Functions of Tim50-cardiolipin interaction:

1. CHANNEL GATING:
   - Tim50 interacts with cardiolipin in the mitochondrial inner membrane to
     PREVENT PROTON LEAKAGE through the TIM23 channel
   - Closes TIM23 channel in absence of preproteins
   - Maintains membrane permeability barrier
   - Presequence-regulated opening

2. RECEPTOR-CHANNEL ASSOCIATION:
   - Cardiolipin REQUIRED to mediate specific receptor-channel associations in
     the TIM23 complex
   - Without cardiolipin, proper Tim50-Tim23 interaction is disrupted

3. BROADER ROLE IN CARDIOLIPIN HOMEOSTASIS:
   - TIMM50 depletion shows similar phenotypes to cardiolipin deficiency
   - Suggests Tim50 plays a role in cardiolipin composition in mitochondria

4. PROTEIN-PROTEIN INTERACTIONS
================================================================================

4.1 DIRECT INTERACTION PARTNERS
-------------------------------

WITHIN TIM23 COMPLEX:

1. TIMM23 (TIM23)
   - Direct interaction via Tim50 β-hairpin
   - Critical residues: R214, K217
   - Functional: Coordinates protein transfer and channel gating

2. TIMM17A/B (TIM17)
   - Core subunit interaction
   - Forms actual translocation channel

3. TIMM21 (TIM21)
   - Regulates lateral sorting pathway (TIM23SORT)
   - Levels minimally affected by TIMM50 deficiency

4. ROMO1 (Mgr2 in yeast)
   - Couples TIM21 to TIM23 core
   - Promotes lateral translocation

PAM COMPLEX COMPONENTS:

5. TIMM44 (TIM44)
   - Links TIM23 to motor complex
   - Levels not affected by TIMM50 deficiency

6. mtHsp70 (mitochondrial heat shock protein 70)
   - Motor protein for complete translocation
   - Levels maintained in TIMM50 deficiency

7. TIMM16, TIMM17 (PAM16, PAM17)
   - Co-chaperones regulating mtHsp70
   - Levels not affected by TIMM50 deficiency

TOM COMPLEX (OUTER MEMBRANE):

8. TOM40 complex
   - Hands off preproteins to TIM23 via TIMM50
   - Forms TOM-TIM23 supercomplex during active import

LIPID INTERACTIONS:

9. CARDIOLIPIN
   - Critical phospholipid regulator
   - Mediates Tim50-Tim23 association
   - Controls channel gating and proton barrier

4.2 EFFECTS OF TIMM50 DEFICIENCY ON PARTNER PROTEINS
----------------------------------------------------

STRONGLY AFFECTED (protein levels decrease):
- TIMM23: Decreased
- TIMM17: Decreased
- Overall TIM23CORE complex levels: Drastically decreased

MINIMALLY AFFECTED (protein levels maintained):
- TIMM21: Minimal to no change
- PAM complex components (TIMM44, mtHsp70, TIMM16, TIMM17): Not affected
- Most TIM23-dependent substrate proteins: Steady-state levels NOT affected
  despite drastic decrease in TIM23CORE

MOST AFFECTED SUBSTRATE CLASSES:
- OXPHOS (oxidative phosphorylation) complex proteins
- MRP (mitochondrial ribosomal protein) machinery
- Laterally sorted proteins via TIM23SORT pathway

7. ENERGY METABOLISM & ATP PRODUCTION
================================================================================

7.1 DIRECT EFFECTS ON OXIDATIVE PHOSPHORYLATION
-----------------------------------------------

TIMM50 deficiency has SPECIFIC EFFECTS on energy metabolism:

COMPLEX V (ATP SYNTHASE):
- Variable Complex V deficiency in TIMM50 patients
- ATP synthase assembly or stability affected
- Reduced ATP production capacity

RESPIRATORY CHAIN:
- Decreased respiratory chain components
- Reduced respiration rates in patient fibroblasts
- Reduced respiration in neurons
- Overall decrease in oxidative phosphorylation

MITOCHONDRIAL MEMBRANE POTENTIAL:
- Lower mitochondrial membrane potential in patient cells
- Compromises driving force for ATP synthesis

CELLULAR ATP LEVELS:
- Reduced cellular ATP levels
- Energy deficit underlies many cellular phenotypes
- Particularly problematic in high-energy tissues (brain, heart)

7.2 COMPENSATORY METABOLIC SHIFTS
---------------------------------

GLYCOLYSIS UPREGULATION:
- Cells with TIMM50 mutations increase glycolytic metabolism
- Compensatory mechanism to maintain ATP levels
- However, insufficient to fully compensate

METABOLIC VULNERABILITY:
- Cells compromise survival in OxPhos-dependent metabolic conditions
- Cannot survive without functional oxidative phosphorylation
- Demonstrates importance of mitochondrial import for energy metabolism

7.3 LINK TO 3-METHYLGLUTACONIC ACIDURIA
---------------------------------------

3-Methylglutaconic acid elevation:

- Marker of mitochondrial dysfunction
- Associated with defects in:
  * Leucine catabolism
  * Mitochondrial membrane integrity
  * Energy metabolism disruption

- Not specific to TIMM50 deficiency but indicates broader mitochondrial stress

7.4 TISSUE-SPECIFIC ENERGY DEMANDS
----------------------------------

BRAIN:
- Extremely high ATP demand
- Neuronal electrical activity requires constant ATP
- Synaptic transmission energy-intensive
- Explains severe neurological phenotype in TIMM50 deficiency

HEART:
- Constant ATP demand for contractile function
- Cardiomyopathy in some TIMM50 patients
- Mouse model shows cardiac hypertrophy with oxidative stress

GENERAL PRINCIPLE:
High-energy demand tissues (brain, heart) are most vulnerable to TIMM50
deficiency, explaining the neurological and cardiac phenotypes.

8. CILIA-MITOCHONDRIA CONNECTION: EMERGING CROSSTALK
================================================================================

8.1 NO DIRECT EVIDENCE FOR TIMM50 CILIARY LOCALIZATION
------------------------------------------------------

IMPORTANT: Current literature does NOT report TIMM50 localization to primary
cilia. TIMM50 appears to be exclusively mitochondrial.

The TULP3-TIMM50 interaction (ipSAE=0.683) is therefore UNEXPECTED and
potentially represents:
1. A novel non-canonical interaction
2. A regulatory connection between ciliary signaling and mitochondrial function
3. Potential false positive requiring experimental validation

8.2 GENERAL CILIA-MITOCHONDRIA CROSSTALK
----------------------------------------

However, there is EXTENSIVE EVIDENCE for functional crosstalk between cilia
and mitochondria:

SPATIAL RELATIONSHIP:
- Mitochondria cluster at the ciliary base
- Critical cellular location for cilia/mitochondria interphase
- Physical proximity enables signaling crosstalk

ENERGY REQUIREMENTS:
- Primary cilia homeostasis and function require HIGH ENERGY
- Cilia are energy-intensive organelles
- Depend on mitochondrial ATP production

IFT (INTRAFLAGELLAR TRANSPORT) ENERGY DEPENDENCE:
- IFT-B mediates anterograde transport via kinesin-II motors (ATP-dependent)
- IFT-A mediates retrograde transport via cytoplasmic dynein (ATP-dependent)
- Motor proteins hydrolyze ATP for movement
- Cilium maintenance energetically costly
- Treatment with rotenone (mitochondrial Complex I inhibitor) DECREASES number
  of ciliated cells in dose-dependent manner
- Both rotenone and oligomycin (ATPase inhibitor) reduce ciliated cells
- Reflected by decreased cellular levels of IFT88 protein

doi:10.1371/journal.pbio.2005651
"The mitochondrial transporter SLC25A25 links ciliary TRPP2 signaling and
cellular metabolism"

REFERENCE: Life Science Alliance 2022; 5(12):e202201505

8.3 BIDIRECTIONAL SIGNALING BETWEEN CILIA AND MITOCHONDRIA
----------------------------------------------------------

MITOCHONDRIAL STRESS → CILIOGENESIS:

mtROS-AMPK-AUTOPHAGY AXIS:
- Mitochondrial stress robustly stimulates ciliogenesis in different cell types
- Mediated by mitochondrial ROS generation
- Subsequent activation of AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK)
- Activation of autophagy
- Promotes primary cilium formation as part of stress response

PROTECTIVE ROLE:
- Mitochondrial stress-induced ciliogenesis protects cells
- Abrogation of ciliogenesis compromises mitochondrial stress-induced autophagy
- Leads to enhanced cell death
- Demonstrates protective signaling axis

DISEASE RELEVANCE:
- In Parkinson's disease models: Primary cilia mediate mitochondrial stress
  responses to promote dopamine neuron survival
- In diabetic nephropathy: Cilia morphology impaired (characterized by ROS
  overproduction and impaired mitochondrial metabolism)
- In astrocytes: Active signaling axis involving mitochondria and primary cilia
  exists; ciliary signaling is part of mitochondrial integrated stress response

REFERENCE: Cell Death & Disease 2019; 10(11):779
doi:10.1038/s41419-019-2184-y
PMID: 31659149 - "Primary cilia mediate mitochondrial stress responses to
promote dopamine neuron survival in a Parkinson's disease model"

REFERENCE: Journal of Neurochemistry 2023; 164(4):601-619
doi:10.1111/jnc.15708
PMID: 36383135 - "Mitochondrial dysfunction compromises ciliary homeostasis

8.4 mTOR PATHWAY: COUPLING ENERGY STATUS TO CILIA
-------------------------------------------------

KEY REGULATORY HUB:

The mTOR pathway couples:
- Cellular energetic status
- Mitochondrial biogenesis
- Cilial length
- Autophagy

Through mTOR and autophagy, mitochondrial function and cellular energetics are
now linked with cilial function.

AMPK-MEDIATED METABOLISM:
- Concomitant cilia-dependent lipophagy and mitochondrial biogenesis contribute
  to ATP production
- Required to support energy-consuming cellular processes:
  * Glucose reabsorption
  * Gluconeogenesis
  * Cytoskeletal remodeling (in renal epithelial cells)
- Demonstrates primary cilium, autophagy, and mitochondrial metabolism are
  STRICTLY INTERCONNECTED

8.5 MITOCHONDRIAL IMPAIRMENT EFFECTS ON CILIA MORPHOLOGY

USING MITOCHONDRIAL INHIBITORS:

8.7 IMPLICATIONS FOR TIMM50-TULP3 INTERACTION
---------------------------------------------

HYPOTHESIS 1: INDIRECT METABOLIC REGULATION
Could TIMM50 import activity respond to ciliary signaling status?
- Ciliary activation → metabolic signals → regulation of mitochondrial protein
  import
- TULP3 might signal cellular metabolic state to mitochondria
- Speculative; requires experimental validation

HYPOTHESIS 2: COORDINATED ORGANELLAR BIOGENESIS
Could ciliogenesis and mitochondrial biogenesis be coordinated?
- Both are energy-expensive processes
- Cells might coordinate their assembly
- TULP3-TIMM50 interaction could be part of coordination mechanism
- Highly speculative

HYPOTHESIS 3: SHARED QUALITY CONTROL PATHWAYS
Could there be shared protein quality control between cilia and mitochondria?
- Both have specialized protein import machinery
- Both require protein quality control
- Possible (but unproven) shared regulatory mechanisms

HYPOTHESIS 4: FALSE POSITIVE
AlphaFold3 prediction with ipSAE=0.683 (medium confidence) may represent:
- Structural similarity without functional interaction
- Requires experimental validation (Co-IP, proximity labeling, functional
  assays)

CRITICAL NOTE:
No published literature currently describes direct TIMM50-TULP3 interaction.
This would be a NOVEL finding requiring careful experimental validation.

9. TULP3 CONNECTION: WHY WOULD IFT CARGO ADAPTOR INTERACT WITH

9.1 TULP3 BIOLOGY: IFT-A CARGO ADAPTOR
--------------------------------------

TULP3 FUNCTION:

TULP3 (Tubby-like protein 3) is a critical adapter for ciliary trafficking:

DOMAIN STRUCTURE:
- N-terminal helix: Binds to IFT-A complex
- C-terminal tubby domain: Binds to PI(4,5)P2 and membrane cargoes

TRAFFICKING MECHANISM (3 steps):
1. CAPTURE: Tubby domain captures cargoes at plasma membrane in PI(4,5)P2-
   dependent manner
2. TRANSPORT: TULP3 N-terminus interacts with IFT-A core to transport cargoes
   into cilium
3. RELEASE: Cargoes released in PI(4,5)P2-deficient ciliary membrane

CARGO TYPES:
Transmembrane proteins:
- GPCRs: GPR161, MCHR1, SSTR3
- Polycystins
- Fibrocystin

Membrane-associated lipidated proteins:
- ARL13B (critical ciliary GTPase)
- ARL13B-dependent cargoes: INPP5E, NPHP3, CYS1

FUNCTIONAL SIGNIFICANCE:
- TULP3 and IFT-A proteins both negatively regulate Hedgehog signaling
- Critical for ciliary GPCR trafficking
- Essential for proper ciliary membrane composition

12. SUMMARY & KEY POINTS
================================================================================

TIMM50 CORE FUNCTION:
- Receptor subunit of TIM23 complex in mitochondrial inner membrane
- Recognizes presequences on proteins destined for mitochondrial matrix and
  inner membrane
- Coordinates protein transfer from TOM to TIM23
- Regulates TIM23 channel gating via cardiolipin interaction
- Prevents proton leakage to maintain membrane potential

DISEASE:
- Mutations cause 3-methylglutaconic aciduria type IX (MGCA9)
- Autosomal recessive mitochondrial disorder
- Seven mutations identified in ten families (geographically diverse)
- Phenotype: Early-onset seizures, severe developmental delay, intellectual
  disability, optic atrophy, cardiomyopathy

MOLECULAR PATHOLOGY:
- Reduced TIM23 complex levels
- Impaired protein import (especially via TIM23SORT)
- Decreased OXPHOS complex proteins
- Reduced ATP production
- Increased ROS
- Neuronal potassium channel depletion → epilepsy

ENERGY METABOLISM:
- Critical for oxidative phosphorylation
- Variable Complex V deficiency in patients
- Cells vulnerable in OxPhos-dependent conditions
- High-energy tissues (brain, heart) most affected

CILIA-MITOCHONDRIA CONNECTION:
- NO direct evidence for TIMM50 ciliary localization
- Extensive general crosstalk between cilia and mitochondria:
  * mtROS-AMPK-autophagy axis regulates ciliogenesis
  * mTOR couples energy status to ciliary length
  * Mitochondrial inhibitors reduce ciliated cells and IFT88 levels
  * Cilia require high ATP for IFT motor proteins
  * Physical proximity at ciliary base

TULP3-TIMM50 INTERACTION:
- AlphaFold3 prediction (ipSAE=0.683, medium confidence)
- NO published evidence for this interaction
- Multiple plausible biological scenarios:
  * Metabolic coordination of ciliary signaling
  * Phosphoinositide signaling connection
  * Shared quality control
  * ROS/calcium signaling bridge
- REQUIRES EXPERIMENTAL VALIDATION (Co-IP, proximity labeling, functional
  assays)
- Could represent novel organellar crosstalk OR structural similarity without
  functional interaction

STRUCTURAL FEATURES:
- Single-pass transmembrane protein
- Large IMS domain with core and presequence-binding sub-domains
- Protruding β-hairpin for Tim23 interaction
- Phosphatase-like fold (function disputed)
- Cardiolipin binding critical for function

PROTEIN INTERACTIONS:
- Direct: TIMM23, TIMM17, TIMM21, ROMO1, TIMM44, cardiolipin
- Affects: OXPHOS complexes, mitochondrial ribosomes, laterally sorted proteins

UNIQUE FEATURES:
- Human TIMM50 has phosphatase activity (yeast does not)
- Cardiolipin-mediated channel regulation
- Tissue-specific vulnerabilities (brain, heart)
