1. PROTEIN OVERVIEW
================================================================================

Gene Name: INTS6 (Integrator Complex Subunit 6)
Alternative Names:
  - DICE1 (Deleted in Cancer 1)
  - DDX26 (DEAD Box Protein 26)
  - INT6

UniProt ID: Q9UL03
Chromosomal Location: 13q14.2-q14.3
OMIM: *604331

Protein Characteristics:
  - Length: 805 amino acids
  - DEAD box protein (Asp-Glu-Ala-Asp motif)
  - Contains von Willebrand factor type A domain
  - Putative ATP-dependent RNA helicase
  - Molecular weight: ~90 kDa

Primary Function: Component of the Integrator complex that associates with
RNA polymerase II C-terminal domain (CTD) to regulate snRNA 3' end processing,
transcription termination, and gene expression.

2. CILIARY CONNECTIONS
================================================================================

2.1 Direct Ciliary Link via INTS13
-----------------------------------
While INTS6 itself has not been extensively studied in ciliopathy contexts,
other Integrator complex subunits have strong ciliary connections:

INTS13 Ciliopathy (PMID: 36168916, Nature Communications 2022):
  - Mutations in INTS13 cause oral-facial-digital syndrome type 2 (autosomal
    recessive ciliopathy)
  - Depletion of INTS13 disrupts ciliogenesis in human cultured cells
  - Causes dysregulation of broad collection of ciliary genes
  - Multiple Integrator subunits (INTS1, INTS8, INTS13) linked to
    ciliopathy-related symptoms

Mechanism:
  - Integrator complex regulates transcription of ciliary genes
  - Transcriptional regulation is the mechanism linking Integrator function
    to ciliogenesis
  - Integrator multiprotein complex associates with RNA Pol II and cleaves
    nascent RNA to modulate gene expression

2.2 Relevance to IFT22 Interaction
-----------------------------------
The predicted interaction between INTS6 and IFT22 (ipSAE=0.583) is intriguing
because:

1. NUCLEAR-CILIARY AXIS: Growing evidence suggests nuclear transcription
   factors and RNA processing machinery communicate with ciliary assembly
   mechanisms

2. IFT22 DUAL LOCALIZATION: While IFT22 is primarily known for intraflagellar
   transport, some IFT proteins shuttle between cytoplasm and nucleus, though
   specific nuclear functions of IFT22 remain largely unexplored

3. TRANSCRIPTIONAL REGULATION OF IFT: INTS6/Integrator complex could regulate
   transcription of IFT or ciliary genes, with IFT22 potentially providing
   feedback signaling

4. NOVEL PATHWAY: This interaction may represent an undiscovered regulatory
   axis between nuclear RNA processing and ciliary protein trafficking

3. INTEGRATOR COMPLEX FUNCTION
================================================================================

3.1 Complex Architecture
------------------------
The Integrator complex consists of 14 subunits (INTS1-INTS14) organized into
functional modules:

MODULES:
  - Cleavage module: INTS4/9/11 (RNA endonuclease activity)
  - Shoulder module: INTS5/8
  - Phosphatase module: INTS6 + PP2A-A + PP2A-C (forms Int-PP2A or INTAC)
  - Ternary module: INTS10/13/14 (nucleic acid binding)

Reference: PMID: 35303060 (Nature Reviews Mol Cell Biol 2022)

3.2 INTS6-Specific Functions within Integrator
-----------------------------------------------

INTS6 as Molecular Adapter (PMID: 32763134, Science 2020):
  - Acts as molecular adapter promoting assembly of PP2A subunits to
    Integrator core
  - Bridges PP2A dimer to INTS5-INTS8 shoulder module
  - Functions analogously to PP2A regulatory B subunit
  - INTS6 deletion results in loss of PP2A components from Integrator
  - INTS8 deletion causes loss of both PP2A and INTS6

INTS6 Protein-Protein Interactions:
  - Direct binding partner of INTS3 (C-terminal regions)
  - Mediates contacts to INTS2 (scaffolding module)
  - Bridges to INTS5-INTS8 shoulder module
  - Weak interaction with shoulder module alone

3.3 snRNA 3' End Processing
----------------------------

Mechanism (Reactome: R-HSA-6814555):
  - Integrator complex recognizes RNA Pol II transcribing U-rich snRNAs
    (U1, U2, U4, U5, U7, U11, U12)
  - INTS9/INTS11 heterodimeric nuclease cleaves pre-snRNA 5' to the 3' box
  - Cleavage releases capped snRNA bound to cap binding complex
  - Coupled with transcription termination

INTS6 Role in snRNA Processing:
  - As DEAD box helicase, may resolve RNA-DNA hybrids in transcription bubbles
  - INTS6 binds DNA:RNA hybrids and R-loops with highest affinity
  - Helps resolve secondary structures during snRNA processing
  - NELF interaction required for proper U1, U2, U4, U5 processing

Reference: PMID: 24531476 (Nature Communications 2014)

3.4 Enhancer RNA (eRNA) Regulation
-----------------------------------

Integrator as eRNA Terminator (PMID: 26550823, Nature 2015):
  - Majority of eRNAs lack poly(A) tail and are cleaved by Integrator
  - Integrator recruited to enhancers and super-enhancers in
    stimulus-dependent manner
  - Global nuclear run-on shows Integrator directs 3' cleavage of eRNA
    transcripts → transcriptional termination
  - Functional depletion of Integrator diminishes signal-dependent eRNA
    induction
  - Abrogates stimulus-induced enhancer-promoter chromatin looping

eRNA Protection Mechanism:
  - m6A modification recruits m6A reader proteins (hnRNPG, YTHDC1)
  - These proteins protect nascent RNAs from Integrator cleavage
  - Loss of m6A modification → recruitment of INTS11 endonuclease →
    premature termination

eRNA Functions in Pause Release (PMID: 35508652, Nature Communications 2022):
  - eRNAs >200 nucleotides with unpaired guanosines bind NELF-A and NELF-E
  - Decoy NELF away from immediate early genes
  - Promotes RNA Pol II pause release into productive elongation
  - eRNAs harness multivalent interactions with NELF to stimulate pause release

Reference: PMID: 36180603 (Nature Reviews Mol Cell Biol 2022)

3.5 Premature Transcription Termination
----------------------------------------

Promoter-Proximal Pausing (PMID: 37995689, Molecular Cell 2023):
  - IntS6 and Integrator phosphatase module tune efficiency of premature
    transcription termination
  - Over-expressed IntS6 titrates PP2A subunits
  - Only affects gene loci where phosphatase activity necessary for
    Integrator function
  - IntS6 functions analogous to PP2A regulatory B subunit

Dual Regulatory Roles:
  - Context-dependent functions in both promoting and restricting
    transcription at different genomic locations
  - Key modulator of RNA Pol II promoter-proximal pausing
  - Regulates transcript elongation

4. RNA POL II PHOSPHORYLATION REGULATION
================================================================================

4.1 The Integrator-PP2A Complex (INTAC)
----------------------------------------

Discovery (PMID: 32763134, Science 2020):
  - Integrator associates with PP2A core enzyme (PP2A-AC) to form INTAC
  - First non-canonical PP2A complex lacking typical PP2A-B regulatory subunit
  - INTS6 replaces regulatory B subunit function

Dual Enzymatic Activities:
  - RNA cleavage (via INTS11 endonuclease)
  - RNA Pol II dephosphorylation (via PP2A phosphatase)
  - These activities are structurally and functionally integrated

4.2 Mechanism of CTD Dephosphorylation
---------------------------------------

Substrate Specificity:
  - INTAC dephosphorylates RNA Pol II CTD at Ser2, Ser5, and Ser7
  - Also dephosphorylates Spt5 (elongation factor)
  - Counteracts Cdk7 and P-TEFb kinase activity

Functional Consequences (PMID: 33086037, Molecular Cell 2020):
  - Integrator binds RNA Pol II CTD
  - Positions PP2A to counteract Pol II phosphorylation and elongation
  - PP2A removes stimulatory phosphorylation from CTD and Spt5
  - Prevents transition to productive elongation
  - Facilitates transcription termination

Structural Insights (PMID: 38538851, Nature 2024):
  - Cryo-EM structures reveal how Integrator-bound PP2A contacts RNA Pol II
  - Structural basis of Integrator-dependent RNA Pol II termination

Key Point: INTS6 DOES NOT HAVE KINASE ACTIVITY
  - INTS6 regulates phosphatase (dephosphorylation) activity
  - It does not directly phosphorylate substrates
  - Instead, recruits PP2A phosphatase to remove phosphate groups

5.1 Integrator Complex Subunits
--------------------------------
Direct Interactions:
  - INTS3: Direct binding partner (C-terminal regions, PMID: 34400606)

----------------------------------

  - PP2A-A (scaffold subunit): Direct interaction
  - INTS6 promotes assembly of PP2A heterodimer
  - Forms non-canonical PP2A complex (first described, PMID: 32763134)

5.3 RNA Polymerase II
---------------------
  - Integrator complex binds RNA Pol II CTD
  - INTS6-PP2A module positions phosphatase near CTD
  - Enables dephosphorylation of Ser2, Ser5, Ser7

5.4 SOSS1 Complex (DNA Damage Response)
----------------------------------------
  - INTS3 (SOSS1 subunit)
  - INIP (INTS3-NABP1 interaction protein)
  - hSSB1 (human single-strand DNA binding protein 1)
  - Forms tetrameric INTS6-SOSS1 complex at DNA damage sites

Reference: PMID: 39445827 (Nucleic Acids Research 2024)

5.5 Senataxin (SETX)
--------------------
  - INTS6 interacts with and recruits Senataxin to double-strand breaks
  - Facilitates resolution of DNA:RNA hybrids/R-loops
  - Critical for DNA damage response

5.6 NELF Complex
----------------
  - Negative elongation factor
  - Interactions required for proper snRNA processing
  - NELF knockdown causes misprocessing of U1, U2, U4, U5 snRNAs

Reference: PMID: 24531476 (Nature Communications 2014)

5.7 Cap Binding Complex
-----------------------
  - Interacts during snRNA 3' end processing
  - Binds capped snRNA after Integrator cleavage

6.2 Overlap with Other Integrator Complex Disorders
----------------------------------------------------

INTS1 Syndrome (OMIM: 618571):
  - Neurodevelopmental disorder with cataracts, poor growth, dysmorphic facies
  - Biallelic INTS1 mutations (recessive)
  - Features: Severe developmental delay, skeletal/facial dysmorphia, axial
    hypotonia, cataracts, intellectual disability, lack of verbal communication
  - Growth retardation, hypertelorism, mild cataracts, uneven teeth
  - PMIDs: 30622326, 28542170, 31428919

INTS8 Syndrome:
  - Biallelic INTS8 mutations (recessive)
  - Severe neurodevelopmental delay, distinctive appearance
  - Neuronal migration defects (periventricular nodular heterotopia)
  - Profound intellectual disability, lack of speech, motor impairment
  - Seizures, facial and limb dysmorphism
  - Similar phenotype to INTS1 syndrome
  - PMID: 28763441

Common Theme:
  - Phenotype overlap among INTS1, INTS6, and INTS8 suggests shared
    pathomechanism
  - Integrator complex integrity critical for brain development
  - Transcriptome disruption likely underlies neurodevelopmental phenotypes
  - PMID: 28542170 (PLOS Genetics 2017)

6.3 Cancer - Context-Dependent Roles
-------------------------------------

Tumor Suppressor Function (Most Tissues):
  - INTS6 originally identified as DICE1 (Deleted in Cancer 1) in 1999
  - Located in 13q14.2 critical region of loss of heterozygosity (LOH)
  - Downregulated in multiple cancers:
    * Non-small cell lung carcinoma
    * Prostate cancer (PMID: 25660097)
    * Nasopharyngeal carcinoma
    * Breast cancer
    * Childhood B-precursor acute lymphoblastic leukemia
    * Hepatocellular carcinoma

Oncogenic Function (Colorectal Cancer):
  - OPPOSITE role in colorectal cancer (PMID: 34508742, 2021)
  - INTS6 expression significantly INCREASED in CRC tissues vs normal
  - Associated with POOR prognosis
  - Downregulation of INTS6 → G1/S arrest, suppressed CRC growth
  - Overexpression of INTS6 → promoted CRC growth

Mechanism in CRC:
  - INTS6 increases phosphorylated AKT (p-AKT) and ERK (p-ERK) levels
  - Activates PI3K/AKT and MAPK signaling pathways
  - Affects c-Myc and CDK2 expression → cell cycle alterations
  - Growth-promoting effect inhibited by AKT and ERK inhibitors

Conclusion:
  - INTS6 has context-dependent functions in cancer
  - Tumor suppressor in most cancers
  - Oncogene in colorectal cancer

7.1 INTS6-SOSS1 Complex at Double-Strand Breaks
------------------------------------------------

Formation (PMID: 39445827, Nucleic Acids Research 2024):
  - INTS6 associates with trimeric SOSS1 complex following DNA damage
  - Forms tetrameric INTS6-SOSS1 complex at DSB sites
  - Components: INTS3, INIP, hSSB1, INTS6

7.2 DNA:RNA Hybrid and R-loop Binding
--------------------------------------

Binding Specificity:
  - INTS6 binds DNA:RNA hybrids with HIGHEST affinity
  - Also binds R-loops (DNA:RNA hybrid structures)
  - Binds single-stranded DNA (ssDNA) in length-dependent manner
  - Tetrameric SOSS1 complex exhibits similar binding pattern with different
    affinities

7.3 Functions at DSB Sites
---------------------------

PP2A Recruitment:
  - INTS6 promotes PP2A recruitment to DSBs
  - Facilitates dephosphorylation of RNA Pol II at damage sites
  - Prevents accumulation of damage-associated RNA transcripts (DARTs)
  - Prevents stabilization of DNA:RNA hybrids at DSB sites

Senataxin Recruitment:
  - INTS6 interacts with and promotes Senataxin (SETX) recruitment to DSBs
  - Facilitates resolution of DNA:RNA hybrids/R-loops
  - Critical for proper DNA damage repair

Autoregulation:
  - Tetrameric INTS6-SOSS1 complex facilitates DNA:RNA hybrid autoregulation
    at DSBs

7.4 C. elegans Studies
----------------------

INTS-6 in DNA Damage Response (PMID: 39575199, 2024):

  - Integrator complex subunits (INTS-3, INTS-6) involved in DNA damage

7.5 Crystal Structure
---------------------

9. BIOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES
================================================================================

9.1 DEAD-Box Helicase Domain
-----------------------------

Structural Features:
  - DEAD motif: Asp-Glu-Ala-Asp (conserved)
  - Two RecA-like domains forming helicase core
  - Conserved motifs Q, I, II (DEAD), IV for ATP binding/hydrolysis
  - von Willebrand factor type A domain

Function as Putative Helicase:
  - ATP-dependent RNA helicase activity (predicted)
  - May resolve RNA-DNA hybrids in transcription bubbles
  - Binds DNA:RNA hybrids and R-loops with high affinity
  - Could unwind RNA secondary structures

DEAD-Box Mechanism (General):
  - Cooperative binding of ATP and RNA induces closed conformation
  - Cleft closure between RecA-like domains
  - ATP hydrolysis required for enzyme recycling, not unwinding per se
  - ADP-beryllium fluoride allows unwinding without ATP hydrolysis
  - ATP hydrolysis enables fast enzyme release and multiple turnovers

Note: Specific ATP hydrolysis and unwinding activities of INTS6 not
extensively characterized, inferred from DEAD-box family properties.

9.2 Nucleic Acid Binding Specificity
-------------------------------------

Hierarchy of Binding Affinity (PMID: 39445827):
  1. DNA:RNA hybrids (HIGHEST affinity)
  2. R-loops (RNA:DNA:DNA structures)
  3. Single-stranded DNA (length-dependent)
  4. Likely also binds RNA (DEAD box proteins are RNA helicases)

Functional Implications:
  - Preferential binding to hybrid structures suggests role in
    transcription-associated processes
  - R-loop resolution important for genome stability
  - ssDNA binding relevant for DNA damage response

11. IFT22 CONNECTION - HYPOTHETICAL MECHANISMS
================================================================================

Predicted Interaction: INTS6 ↔ IFT22, ipSAE = 0.583 (MEDIUM confidence)

11.1 Why is This Interaction Surprising?
-----------------------------------------

Compartment Mismatch:
  - INTS6: Primarily nuclear (RNA processing, transcription)
  - IFT22: Primarily ciliary (intraflagellar transport)
  - Unexpected spatial overlap

Functional Domain Mismatch:
  - INTS6: RNA metabolism, gene regulation
  - IFT22: Ciliary protein trafficking, small GTPase-like function

11.2 Possible Biological Scenarios
-----------------------------------

HYPOTHESIS 1: Nuclear Role for IFT22
  - Some IFT proteins may shuttle to nucleus (not yet well-documented for IFT22)
  - Could participate in transcriptional regulation of ciliary genes
  - Precedent: Other signaling proteins (e.g., GLI transcription factors)
    shuttle between cilia and nucleus

HYPOTHESIS 2: Cytoplasmic Role for INTS6
  - INTS6 may have functions outside nucleus
  - Integrator subunits could dissociate from complex
  - Cytoplasmic INTS6 could interact with IFT22 during ciliary protein
    sorting or trafficking

HYPOTHESIS 3: Transcriptional Feedback
  - INTS6/Integrator complex regulates transcription of IFT genes
  - IFT22 or IFT complex components could provide feedback signal to nucleus
  - Precedent: INTS13 mutations cause ciliopathy, linking Integrator to
    cilia gene regulation

HYPOTHESIS 4: Co-regulation of Protein Synthesis
  - Both proteins could interact at sites of localized translation
  - IFT proteins require precise spatiotemporal expression
  - INTS6 could regulate processing of mRNAs encoding ciliary proteins
  - IFT22 could be involved in trafficking those mRNAs or proteins

HYPOTHESIS 5: Shared Protein Interaction Networks
  - May interact indirectly through shared partners
  - Could be part of larger regulatory network coordinating gene expression
    with ciliary assembly

HYPOTHESIS 6: DNA Damage Response Connection
  - INTS6 functions in DNA damage response (PMID: 39445827)
  - Cilia disassemble during DNA damage
  - Could be coordinating DNA repair with cell cycle and ciliation status
  - IFT22 may signal ciliary status to nuclear DNA repair machinery

11.3 Evidence Supporting Nuclear-Ciliary Axis
----------------------------------------------

1. INTS13 Ciliopathy (PMID: 36168916):
   - Proves Integrator complex directly regulates ciliary genes
   - INTS13 mutations → ciliopathy
   - Depletion of multiple Integrator subunits disrupts ciliogenesis

2. Transcriptional Regulation of Cilia:
   - Many ciliary genes require precise transcriptional control
   - Integrator complex terminates transcription of specific gene sets
   - Could fine-tune ciliary gene expression

3. Cell Cycle Coordination:
   - Cilia assemble/disassemble with cell cycle
   - Integrator regulates cell cycle genes (e.g., c-Myc, CDK2)
   - Nuclear-ciliary coordination required

11.4 Experimental Predictions
------------------------------

If this interaction is functionally significant, we would predict:

1. Co-localization studies:
   - INTS6 and IFT22 should co-localize in some cellular compartment
   - May require specific conditions (e.g., DNA damage, ciliary assembly)

2. Functional assays:
   - INTS6 depletion should affect IFT22 expression or localization
   - IFT22 depletion could affect expression of Integrator-regulated genes

3. RNA-seq analysis:
   - INTS6 knockdown should alter transcription of ciliary/IFT genes
   - IFT22 disruption might affect expression of snRNAs or other
     Integrator targets

4. Biochemical validation:
   - Co-immunoprecipitation to confirm direct interaction
   - Determine if interaction is constitutive or stimulus-dependent

11.5 Literature Gaps
--------------------

Current Knowledge Limitations:
  - IFT22 nuclear functions: UNEXPLORED
  - INTS6 cytoplasmic functions: MINIMAL DOCUMENTATION
  - Integrator complex role in ciliary gene transcription: EMERGING
  - Nuclear-ciliary signaling pathways: POORLY UNDERSTOOD

This interaction, if validated, could represent a NOVEL regulatory axis
connecting nuclear RNA processing with ciliary assembly mechanisms.

12. ADDITIONAL FUNCTIONS

12.1 Dorsal Organizer in Vertebrate Embryogenesis
--------------------------------------------------

Discovery (PMID: 24204286, PLOS Genetics 2013):
  - Maternal-effect ints6 mutation in zebrafish
  - Novel role: confining dorsal organizer to appropriate domain

Mechanism:
  - INTS6 restricts expression of dorsal organizer genes
  - Integrates BMP, Nodal, Wnt, and FGF signaling pathways
  - Maintains spatially restricted BMP signaling
  - Prevents ectopic dorsal fate specification

Phenotype when disrupted:
  - Multiple ectopic axial domains (multiple dorsal organizers)
  - Severe dorsalization
  - Gastrulation defects
  - Embryonic lethality

Significance:
  - Demonstrates Integrator complex role in developmental patterning
  - Links transcriptional machinery to morphogen signaling
  - Conserved function in vertebrate axis formation

12.2 Cell Cycle Regulation
---------------------------

Evidence:
  - INTS6 affects c-Myc and CDK2 expression (PMID: 34508742)
  - Downregulation causes G1/S arrest in colorectal cancer cells
  - Overexpression promotes cell cycle progression

Mechanism:
  - Integrator complex regulates transcription of cell cycle genes
  - Premature termination controls expression levels
  - Affects both transcription initiation and elongation

12.3 Translation Initiation (Non-Integrator Function)
-----------------------------------------------------

Historical Context:
  - INTS6 originally discovered as eIF3 subunit in some contexts
  - Fission yeast INT6 associates with eIF3 core subunits
  - NOT essential for global translation initiation (yeast studies)

Current Understanding:
  - Primary function is Integrator complex component (transcription/RNA
    processing)
  - Translation roles may be minor or species-specific
  - Predominant function in mammals: Integrator complex

  - Upregulated in colorectal cancer (oncogenic role)

13.2 Subcellular Localization
------------------------------

Primary: Nuclear
  - Integrator complex functions in nucleus
  - Associates with RNA Pol II in nucleoplasm
  - Concentrates at sites of active transcription

Nucleoli:
  - May be present in nucleoli (RNA processing)

Chromatin Association:
  - Binds chromatin at active genes
  - Enriched at promoter-proximal regions
  - Also found at enhancers and super-enhancers

DNA Damage Foci:
  - Recruited to double-strand break sites
  - Forms INTS6-SOSS1 complex at DSBs
  - PMID: 39445827

Cell Cycle:
  - Expression and localization may vary with cell cycle
  - Functions in chromosome segregation (yeast)

Potential Cytoplasmic Functions:
  - Largely unexplored in mammals
  - Could have non-canonical roles outside nucleus

  - Combination therapy strategy

Other Cancers:

  - Gene therapy to restore INTS6 expression?
  - Context matters: need cancer-specific strategies

  - Could be target for cancer sensitization to DNA damage

Emerging:

  - Modulating Integrator activity might affect ciliogenesis
  - INTS13 ciliopathy demonstrates Integrator as ciliopathy target

15. SUMMARY AND KEY POINTS
================================================================================

INTS6 is a multifunctional DEAD-box protein with diverse roles:

PRIMARY FUNCTIONS:
  1. Core component of Integrator complex
  2. Molecular adapter recruiting PP2A phosphatase
  3. Regulates RNA Pol II CTD dephosphorylation
  4. Processes snRNA 3' ends (U1, U2, U4, U5)
  5. Regulates enhancer RNA biogenesis
  6. Controls premature transcription termination

ADDITIONAL FUNCTIONS:
  7. DNA damage response (DSB repair, R-loop resolution)
  8. Vertebrate embryonic patterning (dorsal organizer)
  9. Cell cycle regulation
  10. Chromosome segregation (conserved from yeast)

DISEASE RELEVANCE:
  - Neurodevelopmental disorders (haploinsufficiency)
  - Overlapping phenotypes with INTS1/INTS8 syndromes
  - Context-dependent cancer roles (tumor suppressor vs oncogene)
  - Potential ciliopathy connection (via INTS13 and IFT22 interaction)

EVOLUTIONARY CONSERVATION:
  - Conserved from yeast to humans
  - Functional complementation across species
  - Essential for development in all organisms studied

IFT22 INTERACTION:
  - Medium confidence prediction (ipSAE=0.583)
  - Potentially represents novel nuclear-ciliary regulatory axis
  - Mechanism unclear but several plausible hypotheses
  - Requires experimental validation
  - Could link Integrator-mediated gene regulation to ciliary assembly

17. CONCLUSION
================================================================================

INTS6 is an essential, evolutionarily conserved component of the Integrator
complex with critical roles in RNA processing, transcription regulation, DNA
damage response, and development. Recent discoveries link INTS6 to
neurodevelopmental disorders, establishing it as a disease gene of significant
clinical importance.

The predicted interaction between INTS6 and IFT22 (ipSAE=0.583) is
intriguing and could represent a novel regulatory connection between nuclear
RNA processing machinery and ciliary assembly mechanisms. While the functional
significance remains to be validated, several plausible hypotheses exist:

1. Transcriptional feedback: INTS6 regulates IFT gene expression, IFT22
   signals back to nucleus
2. Nuclear role for IFT22: Undiscovered nuclear functions for IFT proteins
3. Cytoplasmic role for INTS6: Non-canonical functions outside Integrator
   complex
4. DNA damage-ciliary coordination: Linking genome integrity with ciliation
   status
5. Indirect interaction via shared regulatory networks

The established connection between Integrator complex (INTS13) and ciliopathies
provides precedent for Integrator-ciliary crosstalk. Further experimental
validation of the INTS6-IFT22 interaction could reveal fundamental insights
into nuclear-ciliary communication.

Key unanswered questions:
  - Does IFT22 have nuclear functions?
  - Does INTS6 have cytoplasmic/ciliary functions?
  - How does Integrator complex regulate ciliary gene expression?
  - What is the pathomechanism of INTS6-related neurodevelopmental disorder?
  - Why does INTS6 have opposite roles in different cancers?

INTS6 remains an active area of research with implications for development,
disease, and fundamental cell biology.
