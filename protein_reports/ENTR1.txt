Regulator 1)

INTERACTION CONTEXT:

- Medium confidence interaction
- Potential functional link between endosomal trafficking and BBSome-mediated
  ciliary GPCR trafficking

================================================================================
1. GENE AND PROTEIN IDENTIFICATION
================================================================================

Official Gene Symbol: ENTR1
Alternative Names: SDCCAG3 (Serologically Defined Colon Cancer Antigen-3)
UniProt ID: Q96C92
Gene ID: 10807 (human)
Chromosomal Location: Human chromosome 12
Protein Family: ENTR1 family

Protein Structure:
- 374 amino acids (human)
- Contains a coiled-coil domain in C-terminal half
- N-terminal domain is necessary and sufficient for basal body localization
  and ciliogenesis

Evolutionary Conservation:
- Conserved across vertebrates (human, mouse, rat, zebrafish)
- Mouse ortholog initially called Sdccag3
- Evidence for conservation across mammalian species
- Limited information on broader vertebrate conservation

================================================================================
2. SUBCELLULAR LOCALIZATION
================================================================================

Primary Localizations:
1. Early endosomes
2. Recycling endosomes
3. Centrosome
4. Ciliary basal body (colocalizes with IFT88 and gamma-tubulin)
5. Midbody during cytokinesis (colocalizes with PTPN13)

Ciliary Context:
- Localizes specifically to the basal body of primary cilia
- Does NOT localize to the ciliary axoneme itself
- Strategic positioning for regulating cargo entry into cilia

References:
- PMID: 27767179 (Scientific Reports 2016)
- DOI: 10.1038/srep35399

================================================================================
3. MOLECULAR FUNCTIONS
================================================================================

A. ENDOSOMAL TRAFFICKING AND RECYCLING

Primary Function:
ENTR1 is involved in endosome-to-plasma membrane trafficking and recycling
of SNX27-retromer-dependent cargo proteins.

Key Cargo Protein: GLUT1 (Glucose Transporter 1)
- ENTR1 regulates GLUT1 trafficking and recycling
- Impacts glucose uptake and cellular metabolism
- Transcriptional regulation of GLUT1 expression

SNX27-Retromer Pathway:
- ENTR1 identified as a retromer-interacting protein through proteomics
- Associates with retromer-decorated endosomes
- Functions as retromer accessory protein
- Depletion causes trafficking defects of retromer-dependent cargo
- Shows cargo-specific effects suggesting functional heterogeneity

Functional Heterogeneity:
- Different retromer accessory proteins show differential and cargo-specific
  effects
- ENTR1 represents specialized branch of SNX27-retromer pathway

References:
- PMID: 25278552 (Journal of Cell Science 2014)
- DOI: 10.1242/jcs.154351

B. CILIOGENESIS AND CILIARY PROTEIN TARGETING

Essential Role in Cilia Formation:
- Plays critical role in formation of primary cilia
- Depletion of SDCCAG3/ENTR1 impairs ciliogenesis
- Decreased expression correlates with:
  * Reduced ciliary length
  * Reduced percentage of ciliated cells
- Rescue experiments: Reexpression of SDCCAG3 restores ciliogenesis

Polycystin-2 (PKD2) Ciliary Targeting:
ENTR1 is essential for proper localization of PKD2 to primary cilia.

Mechanism:
- Depletion of Sdccag3 in mouse IMCD3 cells impairs ciliary localization
  of PKD2
- Effect can be rescued by reexpression of Sdccag3
- Functions in pathway: DLG1 → SDCCAG3 → IFT20 → PKD2 ciliary targeting

DLG1-SDCCAG3-IFT20 Pathway:
- DLG1 (Scribble polarity complex) functions upstream
- DLG1 deficiency reduces SDCCAG3, IFT20, and PC2 in cilia
- SDCCAG3 acts as intermediate between DLG1 and IFT machinery
- Critical for kidney epithelial cell cilia function

IFT88 Interaction:
- Yeast 2-hybrid and pull-down experiments identified IFT88 as SDCCAG3-
  interacting protein
- Both proteins colocalize at centrosomes and cilia basal bodies
- IFT88 is core component of IFT-B complex (intraflagellar transport)
- ENTR1-IFT88 interaction links endosomal trafficking to ciliary transport

Clinical Significance:
Proper PKD2 ciliary localization is essential for preventing autosomal
dominant polycystic kidney disease (ADPKD). ENTR1 dysfunction could
theoretically contribute to cystogenesis.

References:
- PMID: 27767179 (Scientific Reports 2016) - Primary ciliogenesis study
- PMID: 37987012 (EMBO reports 2024) - DLG1-SDCCAG3-IFT20-PKD2 pathway
- PMC: 10659422
- DOI: 10.1038/s44319-024-00170-1

C. CYTOKINESIS AND CELL DIVISION

Role in Cytokinesis:
ENTR1 is involved in regulation of cytokinesis through interactions with
PTPN13 and GIT1.

Protein Complex Formation:
- Forms trimeric complex: ENTR1-PTPN13-GIT1
- Domain interactions:
  * ENTR1 binds PTPN13 via FERM domain
  * ENTR1 binds GIT1 N-terminus via its own N-terminus
  * ENTR1 interacts with both N- and C-terminus of GIT1

Localization During Division:
- ENTR1 colocalizes with PTPN13 at midbody during cytokinesis
- Strategic positioning for regulating final abscission

Functional Context:
The ENTR1-PTPN13-GIT1 complex may regulate membrane dynamics and signaling
during the final stages of cell division, though detailed mechanisms remain
to be elucidated.

D. TNF SIGNALING AND APOPTOSIS

TNFRSF1A Trafficking:
ENTR1 is involved in presentation of TNF receptor TNFRSF1A (TNFR1) on cell
surface.

Functional Impact:
- Regulates cell surface levels of TNFRSF1A
- Modulates TNF-induced apoptosis
- Controls delivery of receptors to lysosomes
- Regulates receptor degradation and signaling

Mechanism:
By controlling endosomal trafficking and recycling of TNFRSF1A, ENTR1
determines cellular sensitivity to TNF-induced apoptotic signals.

Cancer Context:
- Depletion of ENTR1 in HCT116 (colorectal cancer) cells activates
  caspase-8 and caspase-3 mediated apoptosis
- Suggests ENTR1 may protect cancer cells from apoptosis
- Potential therapeutic target

References:
- PMID: 38185601 (Journal of Neuro-Oncology 2024)
- DOI: 10.1007/s11060-023-04527-9

E. FAS/CD95 DEATH RECEPTOR TRAFFICKING

Additional Death Receptor:
ENTR1 controls cell surface levels of Fas (CD95) death receptor.

Function:
- Regulates delivery of Fas to lysosomes
- Controls Fas degradation
- Modulates Fas signaling
- Localized to early and recycling endosomes for Fas sorting

Apoptotic Regulation:
ENTR1 serves as key regulator of death receptor trafficking, controlling
cellular sensitivity to apoptotic signals from both TNF and Fas pathways.

Reference:
- PMID: 31133651 (Nature Communications 2019)
- DOI: 10.1038/s41467-019-11025-y

4. PROTEIN INTERACTIONS AND MOLECULAR PATHWAYS
================================================================================

A. CONFIRMED PROTEIN INTERACTIONS

1. IFT88 (Intraflagellar Transport Protein 88)
   - Location: Centrosome, cilia basal body
   - Method: Yeast 2-hybrid, pull-down experiments
   - Function: Links ENTR1 to IFT machinery
   - Reference: PMID 27767179

2. IFT20 (Intraflagellar Transport Protein 20)
   - Part of DLG1-SDCCAG3-IFT20 pathway
   - Function: PKD2 ciliary targeting
   - Reference: PMID 37987012

3. DLG1 (Discs Large Homolog 1)
   - Scribble polarity complex component
   - Acts upstream of SDCCAG3
   - Regulates SDCCAG3 levels in cilia
   - Reference: PMID 37987012

4. PTPN13 (Protein Tyrosine Phosphatase Non-Receptor Type 13)
   - Forms trimeric complex with ENTR1 and GIT1
   - Interaction via FERM domain
   - Function: Cytokinesis regulation
   - Colocalizes at midbody

5. GIT1 (G Protein-Coupled Receptor Kinase Interacting ArfGAP 1)
   - Forms trimeric complex with ENTR1 and PTPN13
   - Interacts via N-terminus (both proteins)
   - ENTR1 binds both N- and C-terminus of GIT1
   - Function: Cytokinesis regulation

6. SNX27 (Sorting Nexin 27)
   - Part of SNX27-retromer pathway
   - ENTR1 functions as accessory protein
   - Function: Cargo recycling to plasma membrane
   - Reference: PMID 25278552

7. Retromer Complex (VPS26, VPS29, VPS35)
   - ENTR1 identified as retromer-interacting protein
   - Associates with retromer-decorated endosomes
   - Function: Endosomal sorting
   - Reference: PMID 25278552

8. PKD2 (Polycystin-2)
   - ENTR1 regulates its ciliary localization
   - Critical cargo protein for cilia function
   - Reference: PMID 37987012

9. GLUT1 (Glucose Transporter 1, SLC2A1)
   - SNX27-retromer-dependent cargo
   - ENTR1 regulates trafficking and recycling
   - ENTR1 also regulates GLUT1 transcription
   - Metabolic significance

10. TNFRSF1A (TNF Receptor Superfamily Member 1A)
    - ENTR1 regulates surface presentation
    - Controls apoptotic sensitivity
    - Reference: PMID 38185601

11. FAS/CD95 (Death Receptor)
    - ENTR1 controls surface levels
    - Regulates lysosomal delivery
    - Reference: PMID 31133651

B. PATHWAY INTEGRATION

Ciliary Trafficking Pathway:
DLG1 (polarity) → SDCCAG3/ENTR1 (endosomal sorting) → IFT20/IFT88
(intraflagellar transport) → PKD2 ciliary localization →
Mechanosensation/calcium signaling

Endosomal Recycling Pathway:
Cargo internalization → Early endosomes (ENTR1) → SNX27-retromer recognition
→ Recycling endosomes (ENTR1) → Plasma membrane recycling →
Receptor availability

Apoptosis Regulation:
Death receptor (TNF-R1/Fas) → Endocytosis → ENTR1-mediated sorting decision
→ Recycling (survival) vs. Lysosomal degradation (sensitization) →
Apoptotic threshold

Metabolic Regulation:
ENTR1 → GLUT1 trafficking/transcription → Glucose uptake → Glycolysis →
Tumor metabolism (cancer context)

5. DISEASE ASSOCIATIONS
================================================================================

A. CANCER

Colorectal Cancer (Primary Association):
- ENTR1 initially identified as serologically defined colon cancer antigen-3
- Overexpressed in colorectal cancer
- Identified as prognostic biomarker for colorectal mucinous adenocarcinoma

Mechanism in Colon Cancer:
- ENTR1 drives progression through glycolysis regulation
- Regulates GLUT1 expression and glucose metabolism
- Shows causal relationship with colon cancer development
- Machine learning and Mendelian randomization support causality

Reference:
- PMID: 37153768 (Frontiers in Oncology 2023)
- PMC: 10172661
- DOI: 10.3389/fonc.2023.1061785
- 2025 BMC Cancer publication
- DOI: 10.1186/s12885-025-14412-5

Glioblastoma Multiforme:
- ENTR1 promotes tumor growth and invasion
- Inhibits TNF signaling pathway
- Affects cell proliferation, invasion, and chemotherapeutic sensitivity

Reference:
- PMID: 38185601 (Journal of Neuro-Oncology 2024)
- DOI: 10.1007/s11060-023-04527-9

Cancer Mechanisms (General):
1. Metabolic reprogramming: Enhanced glycolysis via GLUT1
2. Apoptosis resistance: Reduced TNF/Fas sensitivity
3. Membrane remodeling: Altered cell surface receptor landscape
4. Survival advantage: Protection from death receptor signaling

B. KIDNEY DISEASE (POTENTIAL)

Polycystic Kidney Disease Risk:
While ENTR1 is not a known PKD gene, its role in PKD2 ciliary targeting
suggests potential involvement:

- Required for proper PKD2 localization to cilia
- Defects could impair mechanosensation
- May contribute to cystogenesis if disrupted
- Functions in DLG1-SDCCAG3-IFT20 pathway essential for renal cilia

No direct human disease association established, but rat ortholog associated
with:
- Adams-Oliver Syndrome 5
- Autosomal dominant intellectual developmental disorder 8
- Autosomal dominant nocturnal frontal lobe epilepsy 5

Note: These rat associations require further validation in human genetics.

C. CILIOPATHIES (THEORETICAL)

Current Status:
- ENTR1 is NOT currently recognized as a ciliopathy gene
- NOT among the 21-28 known Bardet-Biedl syndrome genes
- No direct association with established ciliopathies

Potential for Ciliopathy Involvement:
Given ENTR1's critical roles in:
1. Ciliogenesis regulation
2. Ciliary protein trafficking (PKD2)
3. Interaction with IFT machinery (IFT88, IFT20)
4. Basal body localization

ENTR1 variants COULD theoretically contribute to ciliopathy phenotypes,
particularly:
- Renal cystic disease
- Retinal degeneration (if expressed in photoreceptor cilia)
- Developmental defects

This remains speculative pending human genetic evidence.

D. HUMAN DISEASE DATABASE STATUS

OMIM Entry: *618289
Disease Associations: No direct human diseases associated by orthology or
annotation (per International Mouse Phenotyping Consortium)

DECIPHER: Listed but no specific clinical information available

This suggests ENTR1 variants are either:
1. Very rare in human disease

7. BBS5 CONNECTION AND POTENTIAL FUNCTIONAL RELATIONSHIP
================================================================================

INTERACTION CONTEXT:
- ipSAE = 0.587 (medium confidence)
- Prediction from AlphaFold3
- No prior experimental validation in literature

A. BBSome FUNCTION (Context for Interaction)

BBSome Complex:
- Eight-protein complex (BBS1, BBS2, BBS4, BBS5, BBS7, BBS8, BBS9, BBS18)
- Specialized adapter for ciliary membrane protein trafficking
- Cycles through cilia via intraflagellar transport (IFT)
- Primary function: Removal of membrane proteins from cilia

Key Cargoes:
1. G protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs)
   - Somatostatin receptor 3 (SSTR3)
   - Serotonin receptor 6 (HTR6)
   - Melanin-concentrating hormone receptor 1 (MCHR1)
   - Smoothened (SMO)

2. Recognition mechanism:
   - BBSome recognizes ubiquitin chains (K63-linked)
   - Activated GPCRs become ubiquitinated
   - TOM1L2 (ancestral ESCRT protein) assists ubiquitin recognition
   - Beta-arrestin dependent ubiquitination

B. BBS5 SPECIFIC ROLE

Position in BBSome:
- Core BBSome component
- Essential for BBSome assembly
- Pleckstrin homology (PH) domain
- Beta-propeller structure
- Involved in membrane binding

Function:
- Structural component of cargo recognition platform
- Contributes to BBSome-membrane association
- Required for GPCR trafficking from cilia

C. ENTR1-BBS5 INTERACTION HYPOTHESIS

Convergent Functions:
Both ENTR1 and BBS5 are involved in membrane protein trafficking, but in
different cellular compartments:

ENTR1:
- Endosomal sorting and recycling
- Basal body localization
- Regulates entry of proteins into cilia (PKD2)
- SNX27-retromer pathway

BBS5/BBSome:
- Intra-ciliary trafficking
- Removes proteins from cilia
- GPCR export from ciliary membrane
- IFT-dependent

Potential Coordination Models:

MODEL 1: Sequential Trafficking Pathway
Endosomal recycling (ENTR1) → Basal body delivery → Ciliary entry (IFT) →
Intra-ciliary trafficking (BBSome) → Ciliary exit (BBSome) →
Endosomal return (ENTR1?)

In this model, ENTR1 and BBS5 could cooperate in a complete recycling loop
for ciliary membrane proteins.

MODEL 2: Basal Body Coordination
ENTR1 at basal body could interact with BBSome during:
- BBSome assembly before ciliary entry
- BBSome disassembly after ciliary exit
- Handoff of cargo between endosomal and ciliary trafficking systems

MODEL 3: Receptor Sorting Decision
ENTR1-BBS5 interaction could determine fate of membrane receptors:
- Recycling to plasma membrane (ENTR1 pathway)
- Delivery to cilia (requires both ENTR1 and BBSome)
- Lysosomal degradation (ENTR1-mediated)

MODEL 4: Metabolic/Signaling Coordination
Given ENTR1's role in GLUT1 trafficking and BBSome's role in GPCR
trafficking:
- Coordinate metabolic sensing with GPCR signaling
- Link glucose availability to ciliary signaling capacity
- Integrated regulation of cellular metabolism and ciliary function

D. SUPPORTING EVIDENCE FOR INTERACTION

Spatial Proximity:
- ENTR1 localizes to basal body
- BBSome cycles through basal body region
- Potential for transient interactions during BBSome assembly/disassembly

Functional Complementarity:
- ENTR1: Endosomal sorting → cilia entry
- BBSome: Ciliary sorting → cilia exit
- Together could form complete ciliary membrane protein cycle

Common Regulatory Pathways:
- DLG1-SDCCAG3-IFT20 pathway (ENTR1)
- IFT machinery (BBSome)
- Both connect to polarity complexes

E. EXPERIMENTAL PREDICTIONS

If ENTR1-BBS5 Interaction is Functional:

Prediction 1: Co-depletion effects
- Simultaneous knockdown of ENTR1 and BBS5 should show synthetic/additive
  defects in ciliary protein trafficking
- May affect overlapping cargo proteins

Prediction 2: Shared cargo proteins
- Some membrane proteins may require both ENTR1 (for ciliary entry) and
  BBSome (for ciliary exit)
- PKD2 trafficking could involve BBSome for ciliary turnover

Prediction 3: Localization interdependence
- BBS5 depletion might affect ENTR1 localization or vice versa
- Could disrupt basal body organization

Prediction 4: Disease overlap
- ENTR1 variants could modify Bardet-Biedl syndrome severity
- BBS patient cells might show altered ENTR1 function

Prediction 5: GPCR trafficking
- ENTR1 might participate in endosomal sorting of ciliary GPCRs before/after
  ciliary trafficking
- Could regulate GPCR availability for ciliary entry

F. ALTERNATIVE EXPLANATIONS

The predicted interaction could also represent:

1. False Positive: ipSAE 0.587 is medium confidence, interaction may not
   occur in vivo

2. Indirect Association: Could interact via intermediary proteins not
   captured in binary prediction

3. Transient Interaction: May only occur during specific cell cycle phases
   or signaling states

4. Tissue-Specific: May only be relevant in specific ciliated cell types

G. EXPERIMENTAL VALIDATION NEEDED

To validate ENTR1-BBS5 interaction:

1. Co-immunoprecipitation from ciliated cells
2. Proximity ligation assay (PLA) in situ
3. FRET/BiFC to detect interaction in living cells
4. Structural studies of potential interaction interface
5. Functional studies: cargo trafficking with single/double knockdowns
6. Localization studies: do they colocalize at basal body?

endosomal localization and complementary cargo sorting functions, but direct

13. SUMMARY AND SYNTHESIS
================================================================================

ENTR1/SDCCAG3 is a multifunctional endosomal trafficking regulator with
critical roles in:

Core Functions:
1. SNX27-retromer-dependent cargo recycling (GLUT1, receptors)
2. Ciliogenesis and ciliary protein targeting (PKD2)
3. Cytokinesis regulation (PTPN13-GIT1 complex)
4. Death receptor trafficking and apoptosis (TNFR1, Fas)

Strategic Localization:
- Basal body of primary cilia
- Early and recycling endosomes
- Midbody during cytokinesis

Key Interactions:
- IFT machinery (IFT88, IFT20)
- Retromer complex
- Polarity complex (DLG1)
- Cytokinesis regulators (PTPN13, GIT1)
- Cargo proteins (PKD2, GLUT1, TNFR1, Fas)

Disease Relevance:
- Colorectal cancer (metabolic reprogramming)
- Glioblastoma (invasion, apoptosis resistance)
- Potential role in polycystic kidney disease (PKD2 trafficking)
- Theoretical ciliopathy involvement

BBS5 Connection:
The predicted interaction between ENTR1 and BBS5 (ipSAE 0.587) represents a
novel link between endosomal sorting and BBSome-mediated ciliary trafficking.
This could coordinate:
- Receptor delivery to cilia (ENTR1 at basal body)
- Receptor removal from cilia (BBSome)
- Complete recycling cycle of ciliary membrane proteins
- Integration of metabolic state (GLUT1) with ciliary signaling (GPCRs)

Experimental validation is required to confirm this interaction and elucidate
its functional significance.

Broader Significance:
ENTR1 emerges as a key coordinator at the intersection of:
- Endosomal trafficking
- Ciliary transport
- Metabolic regulation
- Apoptotic signaling
- Cell division

This positions ENTR1 as a central hub integrating multiple cellular processes
essential for epithelial cell function, tissue homeostasis, and cancer biology.
